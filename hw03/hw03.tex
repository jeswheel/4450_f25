\begin{enumerate}
    \item Show that the binomial probabilities sum to 1.
    \item If $X$ is an integer valued random variable, show that the pmf is related to the cdf by:
    $$
    p(k) = F(k) - F(k - 1).
    $$
    \item Let $A$ and $B$ be events, and let $I_A$ and $I_B$ be the associated indicator random variables.
    That is, $I_A = 1$ if event $A$ occurs, and $I_A = 0$ otherwise. Show that:
    $$
    I_{A \cap B} = I_A \times I_B = \min(I_A, I_B),
    $$
    and
    $$
    I_{A \cup B} = \max(I_A, I_B).
    $$
    \item Which is more likely: 9 or more heads in 10 tosses of a fair coin, or 18 or more heads in 20 tosses of the same coin?
    \item Suppose that I receive phone calls as a Poisson process with parameter $\lambda = 2$ per hour.
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item If I take a 10-min shower, what is the probability that the phone rings during that time?
      \item How long can my shower be if I want to ensure that the probability of receiving no calls to be at most $0.5$?
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Let $X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)$. What value of $k$ maximizes the pmf of $X$, $p(k)$?
    
    (Hint: consider the ratio $p(k) / p(k - 1)$. Use this to derive when the function is increasing and when the function is decreasing. Don't forget that $k \in \{0, 1, 2, \ldots\}$). 
\end{enumerate}
